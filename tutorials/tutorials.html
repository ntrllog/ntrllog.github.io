Intro to Coding:

With the exception of HTML/CSS, my goal with the coding tutorials is to introduce basic concepts of programming. Not the language! That's why they're titled "Intro to CS" and not "Intro to Java".

They're designed to be short and straight to the point. I focus only on some of the important concepts that are commonly used in currently popular programming languages and skip over other basic concepts that I think are irrelevant in the early stages of learning or are easy to pick up later (e.g. input, other variable types, casting, switch). I also remove the overhead of setting up the tools and environment.

They're designed to be interactive. Instead of going "Here's how to do this, here's how to do this, ...", I let the user do all the typing.

They're designed to be representative. I introduce a problem to solve and provide resources for figuring out how to solve them. This simluates an actual programming experience in which you learn about your problem and apply/modify it to your current problem. (Except I did the Googling part already).

That being said, these are not complete tutorials and should not be treated as such. The hardest part about learning is the beginning and I hope to overcome that difficulty with these tutorials.

P.S. With the exception of HTML/CSS, the exercises are pretty much all the same.
